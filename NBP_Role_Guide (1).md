# Next Best Product — Role Guide

---

## Business Development Officer (BDO)

The high-volume outbound caller. BDOs work a prioritized daily call list across all product types. They don't own customer relationships — they open conversations. Their job is to move fast: call, pitch, log the outcome, move to the next lead. The platform gives them a scored, ordered list with a pre-written call script and objection handlers so they spend zero time on research. Typically 20-30 leads per day. Measured on contact rate and conversion volume.

---

## Product Specialist

A deep expert in one product category — auto loans, HELOCs, mortgages, business banking, credit cards. They receive leads two ways: directly from the platform when a high-confidence competitive displacement is detected (e.g., a customer paying a competitor 8.1% on an auto loan routes straight to the Auto Specialist), or as a referral from a BDO or RM who has already opened the conversation. Their view is filtered to their product only, with rate comparisons, savings calculations, and product-specific scripts front and center. Typically 15-20 targeted leads per day. Measured on conversion rate within their product.

---

## Relationship Manager (RM)

Owns the full customer relationship. RMs work a portfolio of named customers — typically 100-200 — and are responsible for deepening those relationships over time. They don't work a call list; they work a customer list. The platform shows them which of their customers have signals worth acting on today, ranked by urgency. When an event fires — a customer buying a home, starting a business, receiving a windfall — the RM is notified first and decides whether to handle it themselves or loop in a specialist. Measured on products per customer, portfolio revenue, and attrition rate.

---

## Branch Banker

Reactive and in-person. When a customer walks into a branch, the branch banker needs instant context — what does this person have, what should we offer, why. Their interface is a fast lookup tool: type a name, see the top recommendation and a one-line reason. Not a call list, not a queue. Designed for a 30-second window while the customer is sitting across the desk.

---

## Call Center Agent

Highest volume, lowest relationship depth. Agents handle inbound calls and need the simplest possible interface — customer name, one product recommendation, three talking points, outcome logging. No score breakdowns, no evidence panels. Speed and clarity over depth. Measured on handle time and conversion rate at scale.

---

## Manager / Team Lead

Oversees the operation. The manager sees everything: daily lead volume by type, which alerts are uncontacted, BDO performance by contact rate and conversion, which team members need coaching, and which urgent leads are at risk of aging out. They can assign leads, run campaigns, and monitor the health of the intelligence engine without touching individual calls.

---

## Admin / Platform Owner

Controls the engine itself. Sets the adjacency matrix scores, adjusts event confidence thresholds, toggles products in and out of scope, defines routing rules, monitors model performance, and manages suppression lists. Typically a data science or analytics lead working with the business. Changes here affect every lead generated across every role.

---

## Summary

| Role | Interface | Daily Volume | Measured On |
|---|---|---|---|
| BDO | Prioritized call list + script | 20-30 leads | Contact rate, conversion volume |
| Product Specialist | Product-filtered queue + rate intel | 15-20 leads | Conversion rate within product |
| Relationship Manager | Portfolio customer list + signals | 100-200 customers | Products per customer, attrition |
| Branch Banker | Fast name lookup | Reactive / walk-ins | In-branch conversion |
| Call Center Agent | Name + one rec + talking points | 50-60 calls | Handle time, conversion rate |
| Manager | Full pipeline + team performance | Oversight | Team conversion, alert response time |
| Admin | Engine configuration | Configuration | Model accuracy, pipeline health |

---

*Each role sees the same underlying intelligence. The experience is shaped around how they work.*
